When you’re working on getting your child to comply with this important task, it can be helpful to find a toothbrush that they like. Finding the right toothbrush depends on several factors, including your child’s age and sensitivities.

Here are some tips for finding the right toothbrush for your child:

  • For children under six months, use a wet washcloth to wipe off teeth and gums. After six months, you can start brushing their teeth with a soft-bristled toothbrush using very little fluoride toothpaste.

  • For children under three years old, make sure they spit out any excess toothpaste after brushing. Don’t rinse too thoroughly because young children can swallow too much water.

  • For children between three and six years old, begin using the pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste. Continue to remind them not to swallow excess toothpaste.

  • Once your child turns seven, you can give them an adult-size toothbrush with soft bristles and the appropriate amount of fluoride toothpaste.

You can floss between your teeth by holding the string taught and then gently inserting it into the space between teeth, moving it up-and-down, while carefully removing any food particles that may be there. To maximize the benefits, use both sides of the floss. For example, you should wrap one side around each of your middle fingers – this will make it easier to hold onto; next pull it tight and stick each side in between two teeth (ensuring they are far enough apart), using an up-and-down motion against the wall of each tooth. Make sure that you get under the gum line as this will get rid of bacteria that could cause gum disease.

After flossing, you should also brush your teeth by wetting the toothbrush and then gently rubbing it against the surface of each tooth. Make sure to be thorough but gentle – brushing too hard can damage both your gums and enamel. While you are brushing, you should place the bristles at a 45-degree angle from the gum line and then move them in a circular motion, covering all surfaces of each tooth. When doing this make sure to brush up on top/outside surfaces and down on bottom/inside surfaces to ensure that they are properly cleaned. Finally, rinse with water after for a complete cleanse!

Below are some of how your child’s brushing routine should be like:

  1. Use a pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste. Too much toothpaste is not good for your child’s teeth. As mentioned earlier, kids often swallow too much toothpaste and this may lead to fluorosis which is considered as an irreversible condition in some cases. The American Dental Association recommends using a ‘pea-sized’ amount since this is what most kids can handle when brushing their teeth by themselves without swallowing excess amounts of it or spitting out the excess before finishing up their brushing routine. Fluoride used nowadays allows the enamel to repair itself almost instantly after being exposed to acidic elements from food and drinks that we consume throughout the day.

  2. Tooth brushing should begin at least two minutes after eating or drinking something sweet.
    Children are not able to properly clean their teeth the moment they have just eaten food with sugar in it yet. This is because of their front teeth which are still developing and depending on how much sugar your child eats, these teeth may erode very quickly! It’s also best to brush their teeth before going out for recess at school since most kids tend to munch on junk food while they’re playing during that time. Usually by the time you get home, having some healthy snacks will be more appropriate for them until it’s time to brush their teeth again.

  3. Have children start using an electronic toothbrush when they are 7 years old. By this age, children can handle more than just a manual toothbrush that you can buy from the store for about $1.00, similar to how it is in some other countries.
    Electronic toothbrushes are great because there’s no effort needed on your child’s part and they won’t be able to cause any damage by brushing too hard or incorrectly. This way, parents can monitor your child’s brushing routine every day so if their brushing technique is not good enough, it will be easier to correct them and teach them how to properly brush their teeth without causing unnecessary harm to their mouths as opposed with using a manual toothbrush where even the slightest move of the wrist could spell trouble for sensitive gums and teeth!

  4. Always remember that twice a day is enough to have a sparkling set of teeth!
    Experts say that children need to brush their teeth for about 2 minutes twice a day, just as how it is for adults. Remember that the time spent brushing the teeth is not important but it’s more important to focus on brushing correctly and thoroughly so that there won’t be any leftover food particles trapped between your child’s gums and teeth once they are done brushing. If you still think that a child needs more time brushing their teeth, ask them to brush using incremental periods such as 30 seconds extra every week until they reach around 5 minutes of continuous brushing…

  5. Teach your kids how to floss properly even from an early age!
    Flossing should start at least 3 months after your child’s first dental visit. When flossing, be sure to get it between their teeth since brushing can’t always reach these spots properly by itself. If you are not sure how to floss your child’s teeth, ask for tips from your dentist instead of looking up videos on YouTube since there are still some people who think that only adults should know how to floss correctly!

  6. Don’t let kids sleep with a bottle containing water or other sweetened liquids in the bed! This is one of the worst habits that parents can encourage their children to do even if this might seem harmless at first glance. As mentioned earlier, sleeping with a bottle containing liquid could result in tooth decay which is why you should make sure your child understands the importance of this.

  7. Don’t forget to schedule your child’s next dental appointment at least twice a year, especially after their first visit!
